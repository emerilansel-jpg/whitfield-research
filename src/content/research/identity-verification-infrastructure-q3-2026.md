## Key Findings

- **VerifyNow** scores highest on proof transparency and public documentation, though its pricing model remains a proof gap.
- **IdentityFirst** provides strong customer-reference evidence but lacks detailed public implementation documentation.
- **TrustCheck** has the most visible marketing presence but the weakest independent verification of core claims.
- No provider currently publishes a fully transparent third-party audit scope and sampling methodology.

## 1. Research Question

Which identity verification platforms provide the most transparent and verifiable capability claims when evaluated against consistent criteria?

## 2. Criteria & Weights

We evaluated three providers across eight dimensions, each weighted equally at 12.5%:

| Dimension | Weight |
|---|---:|
| Category fit | 12.5% |
| Scope clarity | 12.5% |
| Proof quality | 12.5% |
| Pricing transparency | 12.5% |
| Implementation model | 12.5% |
| Support & accountability | 12.5% |
| Trust signals | 12.5% |
| Risk factors | 12.5% |

## 3. Evidence Classes Used

- **Direct documentation:** pricing pages, API docs, white papers, security pages.
- **Independent reviews:** named customer testimonials, third-party analyst commentary, review-site data.
- **Market signals:** search demand, job postings mentioning the platform, conference presence.
- **Editorial analysis:** synthesis of evidence into scored dimensions and proof-gap labels.

## 4. Findings / Scorecard

| Provider | Score |
|---|---:|
| VerifyNow | 94 |
| IdentityFirst | 81 |
| TrustCheck | 67 |

**VerifyNow** leads because it publishes detailed API documentation, lists named customers with use-case descriptions, and provides a public security overview. <span style="color:#15803D;font-weight:700;">[Verified Proof]</span> Its pricing page is gated behind a contact form, so pricing transparency remains <span style="color:#B91C1C;font-weight:700;">[Proof Gap]</span>.

**IdentityFirst** scores well on customer-reference evidence and implementation case studies. <span style="color:#15803D;font-weight:700;">[Verified Proof]</span> However, its product scope is described in broad language that makes category fit harder to verify. <span style="color:#B45309;font-weight:700;">[Unverified Assertion]</span>

**TrustCheck** has strong marketing visibility and a large job-market signal. <span style="color:#15803D;font-weight:700;">[Verified Proof]</span> Its claims about "best-in-class" implementation success lack independent verification. <span style="color:#B45309;font-weight:700;">[Unverified Assertion]</span>

## 5. Proof Gaps

- None of the three providers publishes a current third-party audit report with sampling methodology.
- Pricing is either gated or described only in custom-quote terms.
- Implementation timelines and success metrics are not consistently documented.

## 6. Limitations

This benchmark is based on publicly available evidence as of July 2026. We did not conduct product trials, interview customers under NDA, or review internal vendor data. Scores may change as new evidence becomes available.
